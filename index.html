Course Syllabus

Course Description

This course supports DMA graduate students in developing a body of work while pursuing their MFA degree. Instruction takes the form of one-on-one tutorials scheduled between the student and the instructor. Each student will work with the instructor to develop an independent research plan for the quarter, and the instructor will review and critique the student's work continually throughout the term. Tutorials operate independently of students' thesis committees and faculty advisors.

Studio Meetings

Students will meet with the instructor 4–5 times throughout the term. Each meeting will last one hour. Meetings must be pre-scheduled at least one month in advance and may be modified if deemed necessary and appropriate by both the instructor and the student. A documented emergency or illness is the only acceptable excuse for a student's absence. If the instructor is unable to attend a scheduled meeting, they will reschedule within one week.

Grading

80%Biweekly Studio Meetings — active participation, preparedness, and engagement with the proposed research plan. Minimum of four studio visits required.
20%Project Planning & Reflection Assignments (two assignments, 10% each)

Assignments

Studio / Student Questionnaire — Week 1Define research interests, establish goals and expectations for the term, and outline preferred approach to critique and feedback. Complete before the first studio meeting.
Final Reflection & Portfolio Survey — Week 10Reflect on research development, evaluate studio working methodologies, identify potential directions for future work. Portfolio due alongside reflection by June 8, 2026 (first day of final exams).

Learning Objectives

  • Become more knowledgeable about theories and techniques used in contemporary media art discursive practices.
  • Redefine skills in discussing their work.
  • Build rhizomatic understandings of prior art practices in relation to their work.

Land Acknowledgement

The University of California, Los Angeles occupies the ancestral, traditional, and contemporary lands of the Tongva and Chumash peoples. Our ability to gather and learn here is the result of coercion, dispossession, and colonization. We are grateful for the land itself and the people who have stewarded it through generations.

Commitment to Inclusive Excellence

My goal as your instructor is to support you in developing your voice as creative stewards. I view the studio as a space of play and freedom — a place where we can challenge psychic, social, political, and cultural boundaries. Creating a supportive environment for learning and critique requires a collaborative effort between students and the instructor.

Students with Different Needs

Students with a disability or health-related issue who need accommodations should make an appointment with the instructor as soon as possible. Visit the UCLA Center for Accessible Education within the first two weeks of the quarter so appropriate arrangements can be made. All information and documentation is confidential.
